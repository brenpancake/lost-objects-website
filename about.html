The Feeling

Everyone knows
what it feels like
to be lost.

Lost in a career that doesn't quite fit. Lost in an industry that feels impossible to break into. Lost in the gap between where you are and where you know you could be. Lost on social media, posting into a void and wondering why nothing's moving.

We know that feeling intimately. Not as an observation — as lived experience. Before Lost Objects existed, before we built anything, we were both navigating our own versions of that disorientation.

The strange thing about feeling lost is that it can make you feel completely alone — even when everyone around you is feeling the same thing.

That's the feeling we started from. It's also the feeling we refuse to leave our clients in.

Scene 02
How We Found Each Other
West Hollywood,
Los Angeles

Two lost people
who found each
other first.

Kyra and Brendan didn't meet with a plan. They met the way most meaningful things happen — at a moment when neither of them was quite sure which direction they were heading in.

Kyra was building her acting career, navigating the specific kind of uncertainty that comes with being a working artist in Los Angeles — talented, disciplined, but unsure how to make herself visible in the right rooms. Brendan was deep in the film world, directing, producing, running Filmmakers Academy — and watching talented people struggle with the same invisible problem over and over again: they were extraordinary at their craft and nearly invisible online.

They recognized something in each other. A shared belief that creativity and strategy weren't opposites. That the industry deserved better than generic social media advice from people who'd never been on a set. That the gap between brilliant work and the audience it deserved wasn't inevitable — it was fixable.

Together, they stopped feeling lost. And they realized that was worth building something around.

Lost Objects started not as a business plan, but as a belief: that the people doing the most interesting creative work in the film industry needed a partner who actually understood their world. Someone who'd been in it. Someone who knew what it felt like to wonder if anyone was watching.

Scene 04
What We Actually Believe

Being lost isn't
a weakness.
It's a starting point.

The best creative work we've ever seen comes from people who are deeply in their craft and genuinely uncertain about everything else. That's not a flaw. That's actually what happens when you care enough about something to go all the way into it.

The problem isn't that you're lost. The problem is that you're trying to navigate social media alone, without a map, without anyone who speaks your language.

At Lost Objects, we don't believe in generic strategy. We don't have a template we apply to everyone. We listen first. We ask questions most consultants don't bother to ask. We learn who you actually are before we figure out who you should be online.

We built this agency specifically for the film industry because we are in the film industry. We don't have to learn your vocabulary. We already speak it. And that changes everything about the work we do together.

When you feel lost — in your career, in your brand, in your social presence — we'll be there. Not to hand you a framework. To actually help you find your way.

Scene 05
Why It Exists

Lost Objects
was never
just a business.

It was a decision Kyra and Brendan made together to stop watching talented people disappear into obscurity because they didn't know how to be visible online. It was a response to a gap they kept seeing. It was, honestly, a little bit of an act of defiance against the idea that social media has to feel hollow and performative.

The name says everything. We are all lost objects at some point — people, brands, ideas that haven't been found yet. The work we do is about finding them. Making them visible. Getting them in front of the rooms they deserve to be in.

We're selective about who we work with not because we're precious about it — but because we go deep. When we take on a client, we take them seriously. We show up. We don't outsource the thinking. We don't apply a template and call it done.

We built Lost Objects to be the agency we wished existed when we were navigating our own uncertainty. The one that actually understood our world. The one that didn't make us feel like a problem to be solved.

When you feel lost,
we'll help you feel
found.

That's not a tagline. That's the whole thing. It's why we built this. It's why we're selective about who we work with. It's why we go deep instead of wide. Because we remember what it felt like to not have someone in our corner who actually understood our world — and we decided to be that for someone else.

— Kyra & Brendan, West Hollywood
